The Problem with Current Social Media

Modern social platforms are architecturally designed to prevent the very coordination needed to hold them accountable.

Current Social Media Problems

Current platforms optimize for engagement, not collective action. This creates systemic issues that prevent genuine coordination:

  • Algorithmic suppression and shadow banning
  • Engagement-optimized content that triggers division
  • Visibility silos that prevent coordination
  • Amplification systems that create hierarchy
  • Pay-to-play attention markets
  • Echo chambers and tribal fragmentation
  • Untransparent algorithmic decision-making

Collective's Solution

We've built a platform that prioritizes collective action over individual engagement:

  • Transparent visibility into who's being seen and why
  • Real-time collective momentum visualization
  • Coordinated action tools and frameworks
  • Community-focused initiative organizing
  • Zero algorithmic suppression or shadow bans
  • Bridge-building across differences
  • Measurable collective impact tracking
